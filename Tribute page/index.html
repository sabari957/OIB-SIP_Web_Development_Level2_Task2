Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

1931 – 2015

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. He played a leading role in the development of India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs and is popularly known as the “Missile Man of India.”

Early Life

Dr. Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He came from a humble background and worked hard from a young age to support his education. His dedication and curiosity led him to become one of India’s greatest scientists.

Achievements

  • Key contributor to India's space and missile programs
  • Played a vital role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests
  • Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007)
  • Recipient of Bharat Ratna (India’s highest civilian award)

Inspiration

Dr. Kalam inspired millions of students through his speeches and books. He believed in dreaming big and working hard to achieve success.
